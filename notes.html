Type Field Note Theme Learning Science Stage Developing

"Good content" and "good learning design" are not the same thing

A 60-minute lecture on Agile can cover the topic thoroughly and still fail every item on a resource-selection checklist — no retrieval, no authentic examples, no path to transfer. Content quality and learning design quality are separate axes, and most review processes only check the first one.

What am I currently thinking?

That most "content review" in EdTech is actually just a fact-check. It rarely asks whether the format helps anyone remember, understand, or transfer what's inside it.

Type Connection Theme Culture, Design Stage Captured

Same information, different reality

Two students get identical scholarship information. One thinks "I should apply." The other thinks "I don't belong here." First-gen, rural, no network versus urban, connected, with mentors already in place — the information was never the variable that mattered.

What does it connect to?

Every "just give them the resources" solution I've seen in access-to-opportunity work. Resources without positioning rarely close the gap they're meant to close.

Type Reading Note Theme AI Stage Captured

AI can retrieve; it can't yet judge

AI can retrieve, generate, automate, summarize, translate. What it doesn't do is judge and evaluate, relate and empathize, navigate ambiguity, create meaning, or ask a genuinely better question. The line isn't "knowing vs. not knowing" anymore — it's knowing vs. learning.

Where might I use this?

As the actual organizing frame for an AI-literacy curriculum, instead of a unit on "how AI works" bolted onto the existing syllabus.
